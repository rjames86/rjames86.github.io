Ryan M's Biking Adventures

Day 4 - New Meadows, ID to Oxbow, OR

Track my route here

The day started off getting a ride up to McCall to get my bike fixed. Everything went very well there. The repair was super cheap and the guy even did an alcohol rub on my back rotor and cleaned my chain.

The ride started by riding the 10 miles back to where I needed to start. Only then did I realize I didn't have my sunglasses. I went back to the BnB where I was staying and they couldn't find them anywhere. I had given up and decided I should just keep going when I looked off the other direction and saw them lying in the grass. I just have dropped them when I wheeled my bike over to load it in the truck. Second good luck of the day!

The roads were great. Not too much climbing and hardly any traffic.

I was loaded up with trail mix and snickers for the ride. Food of champions. Seriously though. It's great food to snack on while you ride.

There was a small convenience store literally in the middle of no where that advertised sandwiches. It turned out to be amazing.

The views coming down into Brownlee Dam were amazing. Some of the best I've seen on the trip so far. Also helped that it was on a descent while I was enjoying the view.

Just as I was about to go in to Oxford and stop for the night my rear tire went flat. Not great. I spent some extra time finding where the puncture was and decided to just patch it to save a tube. I rode on and the tire was still slowly leaking. I inflated it a few times until I got to camp so I could spend more time seeing what happened.

The campground where I stayed was fantastic. Nice grass and amazingly clean showers. When I got back to the shower it was already too dark to work on the tire but I then noticed my front tire was flat! After talking to a bunch of people, it sounds like there is a kind of thorn in this area that can be nasty. That would make sense from what I found in the tire and the fact I had multiple punctures on both tubes.

Tonight was my first night of camping. Everything went well. I wasn't cold and slept comfortably, with the exception of someone nearby who was snoring and I thought it was a bear.
