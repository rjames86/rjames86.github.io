Day 5 - Oxford to Baker City, OR

Track my route here

The morning was by far the best part of my day. A bunch of people came over to see what I was up to and check out my bike. The people camping next to me offered to make me breakfast and hang out with them. I couldn't say no to coffee, sausages and sourdough pancakes. It did mean that I didn't hit the road until 9am, but it was worth it.

The rest of the day was hot and boring. Nearly 95 the entire day and there was very little time I didn't spend slogging uphill. The scenery mostly consisted of brown rolling hills.

This is the only photo I managed to take just before getting into Baker City.

I stopped at a restaurant in Baker City to eat. The guys sitting by me had just done a couple-day tour and chatting with me when some random guy walked up to me. He said that there was a new bike hostel that had just opened if I'm looking for a place to stay. I really wanted to keep riding, but since I knew it was nothing from uphill here too, I decided to stop early. I'm so glad I did. For only $25 I got a great outdoor shower, laundry, food and a bed. There was a garden to pick vegetables, stone fruit, and even bike tools if I needed them. If you're ever in the area, certainly check out the Baker City Bike Hostel!
