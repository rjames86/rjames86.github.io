Day 12/13 - Klamath to Laytonville, CA

Track my route here

No update yesterday. Wasn't too much to report back. It was a good day of riding. I had to stop fairly early and get breakfast since Klamath had no restaurant or store. The redwoods were amazing. I got lucky and got to go ahead of a long line of cars in a construction zone. It was a fairly steep pass, but I was completely alone with no cars. There was still a lot of fog at 5pm but the sun was starting to poke through. It made for a great end of the day.

I also got to see a buddy while going through McKinleyville who I had met while riding the Tour of the Unknown Coast. He and his wife were nice enough to make me some food and chat for a while.

Today started off great. I woke up at 5 and so I was on the road by 6:30. It was dark, quiet and foggy. Going through Avenue of the Giants alone was pretty spectacular. No cars for the first 2-3 hours of the ride.

At around 12 I was doing a lot of climbing with very little shade and the temperatures were getting over 100. I was pretty miserable. With the heat along with pretty busy traffic, I was ready to call it quits. I was close to Ukiah and so my buddy Joe offered to come get me and let me stay at his house. He fed me, cleaned up my bike a little, and even offered to make me breakfast in the morning at 5am! What a guy!

Tomorrow is the final day. It wouldn't be official without having to ride a solid 115 miles. I'm excited to be back home.
