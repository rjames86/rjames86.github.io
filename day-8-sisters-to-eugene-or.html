Ryan M's Biking Adventures

Day 8 - Sisters to Eugene, OR

Track my route here

I think today may have been the prettiest ride I've done to date. My morning started off by stopping at a bike shop and getting proper tubes and a new pair of socks. I then went to Sisters Coffee Company and grabbed some breakfast.

The ride began with a fairly gradual 10-mile climb over McKenzie Pass. It was long, but in the trees and quiet. Once I started getting to the top, the entire scenery changed.

People had talked about the lava fields, but I had no idea what to expect. It was like being on another world. Just black lava rock for as far as you could see. The photos sadly don't do it justice. I haven't spent much time stopping to enjoy the scenery, but this was one of the few times where I just had to stop and look at everything around me.

The descent was mostly fun and occasionally terrifying. Cars going up the pass thought it would be a good idea to cut blind corners and I had a few very close calls with oncoming traffic while easily in my own lane.

The scenery was what you assume Oregon looks like. Very green and lots of trees. The rest of the ride was either flat or a slight negative grade and so the next 80 miles just flew by. I got into Eugene around 6:30pm, got some Thai food and fell asleep. Tomorrow I'll be on the coast!
