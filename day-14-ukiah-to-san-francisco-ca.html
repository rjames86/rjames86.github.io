Day 14 - Ukiah to San Francisco, CA

Track my route here

I’m home! The last day was a big push. It ended up being my second longest day of the trip at 132 miles.

I got up at 5am and Joe had already made me an awesome breakfast of pancakes, eggs, bacon and hash browns. We packed up and hit the road by 5:45. He didn’t have to be at work until 8:30 and so he joined me for the first 15 miles of the ride which was great.

The ride was mostly flat all the way from Ukiah to Petaluma. After that, some climbing started and it became all familiar territory. I’m not sure if that ended up making the ride easier or harder since I knew every hill and every part of the road.

I was getting pretty tired by the time I got to Rancho Nicasio, but I had already managed to put in 100 miles for the day. It was 103 out and all I wanted to do was get home. I ended up getting into the city right at around 3:45pm. Not bad for the mileage I put in. I’m very glad to be done. My legs were really started to get tired and the long days on the bike were getting old. I had a great time. Met some amazing people and really enjoyed the entire trip.

Here are some things I observed and learned during the trip:

  • Women drivers don’t wave. I thought this one was really weird. I had a lot of time to watch people as they passed me. Even when I went out of my way to wave, women would never wave back. From what I can remember, I think only one woman waved. Motorcyclists waved most often. Even when they were passing me going the same direction. Thumbs-up were always the most motivating. I think I only got 3-4 the entire trip, but they always provided a little bit of joy every time I saw one.
  • Trash. Beer cans were by far the thing I saw most often. Specifically Bud Light and Coors. After that it was probably energy drinks. And lastly, bottles of pee. At least I’m pretty sure that’s what they were. I was surprised how many bottles of yellow liquid I saw on the roads. California had very little trash on the roads. However, I did see a lot of empty CO2 cartridges. Not really sure what to make of that.
  • Most drivers are nice. I had some pretty narrow shoulders during the ride. Even big trucks did their best to give me some space.
