Ryan M's Biking Adventures

Day 6 - Baker City to Mitchell, OR

Track my route here

The hostel I stayed at was just amazing. Nice cozy bed, laundry, beer and stone fruit from the tree outside. I was so tired last night that I couldn't keep my eyes open at 8pm. I knew if I went to sleep that early, I could probably get up early and ride. So that's what I did.

My alarm went off at 4:30am. I made a big breakfast and coffee and got ready to go. I was on the road by 5:15am. It was brisk but didn't feel too cold for the first few miles. After a while I decided to look and see what temperature it was. It was 33. That's why I was getting progressively colder. Over the next 10 miles I eventually had put on all my articles of clothing. My fingers and feet were still uncomfortably cold. I was riding on flat roads, but couldn't go faster than 10-11mph otherwise I'd start shivering.

Once the sun came out, the temperature almost instantly rose to 50. I couldn't have been happier. I finally started feeling good and prepared myself for the three passes that were coming up.

This is the top of the third pass. They weren't terribly difficult, but it's still 3 passes in a row.

The day was far prettier than the previous, which made it enjoyable to ride again. The temperatures never got too high. The only battle I had was constant headwinds. Nothing is more frustrating than having to pedal downhill just to maintain speed.

I reached Dayville at around 4pm. At this point I was at 110 miles for the day and still felt pretty good. The mercantile in town (of 149 people) had a full bike shop. I was in desperate need of tubes. They somehow had the exact size I needed, but only after I bought them did I realize they had the wrong valve stem (who uses 700x32mm shraders??). I put them back and decided it was safer to carry some that were a size too small just in case I needed them.

Mitchell was 32 miles away, all up hill. I knew it would be tough, but figured I could get it out of the way tonight instead of in the morning.

Three hours later I finally made it to the Spoke'n Hostel in Mitchell. Pretty tired after a 150 mile day with 3 big climbs and one 20 mile uphill slog.
